# Customer Retention & Churn Analysis Dashboard

## Project Overview

This project analyzes customer retention and churn behavior using Power BI. The objective was to identify churn patterns, retention drivers, and opportunities to improve customer loyalty.

## Tools Used

- Power BI
- CSV Dataset
- Data Cleaning & Transformation

## Dashboard Metrics

- Total Customers: 7,043
- Active Customers: 5,174
- Churned Customers: 1,869
- Churn Rate: 26.54%

## Key Findings

- Month-to-Month contracts showed the highest churn levels.
- Fiber Optic customers experienced the highest churn.
- Electronic Check users recorded the highest churn among payment methods.

## Business Insights

Insight 1: Customer Churn is a Significant Business Challenge

The company has a churn rate of 26.54%, meaning more than one in four customers eventually leave the service. This represents a substantial revenue risk and highlights the importance of retention-focused strategies.

Insight 2: Month-to-Month Customers Are Most Likely to Leave

Customers on Month-to-Month contracts experience the highest churn levels, with approximately 2,220 customers in this category.

This suggests that customers without long-term commitments are significantly more likely to cancel their subscriptions.

Insight 3: Fiber Optic Customers Show Higher Churn

The Fiber Optic Internet Service segment recorded the highest churn levels, affecting approximately 1,799 customers.

This may indicate:

Pricing concerns
Service quality issues
Customer expectations not being met
Strong competition in the fiber market
Insight 4: Electronic Check Users Have the Highest Churn

Customers using Electronic Check as their payment method recorded the highest churn levels, affecting approximately 1,294 customers.

This may suggest:

Less convenient payment experiences
Lower customer engagement
Different customer behavior compared to automated payment users
Insight 5: Strong Customer Base Remains

Despite churn challenges, the company retains over 5,000 active customers, indicating that many customers continue to find value in the service.

This provides an opportunity to study retained customers and replicate successful retention factors.

## Recommendations

Recommendation 1: Convert Month-to-Month Customers to Long-Term Contracts

Offer incentives such as:

Discounted annual plans
Loyalty rewards
Contract upgrade promotions

Reducing dependence on month-to-month subscriptions could significantly lower churn.

Recommendation 2: Investigate Fiber Optic Customer Experience

The business should review:

Service reliability
Network performance
Customer support quality
Pricing competitiveness

Customer surveys can help identify specific pain points.

Recommendation 3: Encourage Automatic Payment Methods

Promote:

Credit card payments
Bank transfers
Auto-payment options

through discounts or loyalty rewards.

Customers using automated payment methods often demonstrate higher retention rates.

Recommendation 4: Launch Early Churn Prevention Campaigns

Use the dashboard to identify high-risk customers and:

Send retention offers
Provide personalized support
Offer plan adjustments before cancellation occurs
Recommendation 5: Monitor Churn Monthly

Management should regularly review:

Churn rate
Contract trends
Service performance
Customer lifetime metrics

to identify risks before they affect revenue.
