# Customer Retention & Churn Analysis Dashboard

## Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing customer retention and churn behavior using Power BI. The objective was to identify churn patterns, customer retention drivers, customer lifetime trends, and opportunities to improve long-term customer loyalty.

The analysis was conducted using a customer subscription dataset containing customer demographics, service information, payment methods, contract types, and churn status. Through data visualization and business intelligence techniques, the dashboard provides actionable insights to support customer retention strategies and revenue growth.

## Tools Used

* Power BI
* CSV Dataset (Telco Customer Churn Dataset)
* Data Cleaning and Transformation
* Data Visualization
* Business Intelligence and Analytics

## Dashboard Metrics

* Total Customers: 7,043
* Active Customers: 5,174
* Churned Customers: 1,869
* Churn Rate: 26.54%

## Key Findings

* Month-to-Month contracts recorded the highest churn levels.
* Fiber Optic customers experienced the highest churn among service types.
* Electronic Check users recorded the highest churn among payment methods.
* More than one in four customers eventually left the service.
* The company maintained a strong base of over 5,000 active customers.

## Business Insights

### Customer Churn Represents a Significant Business Challenge

The company recorded a churn rate of 26.54%, indicating that customer retention remains a critical area requiring strategic attention.

### Month-to-Month Customers Are Most Likely to Leave

Customers using Month-to-Month contracts showed the highest churn levels, suggesting that customers without long-term commitments are more likely to cancel services.

### Fiber Optic Customers Experience Higher Churn

Fiber Optic customers recorded the highest churn levels, which may indicate service quality concerns, pricing issues, customer expectations, or market competition.

### Electronic Check Users Have the Highest Churn

Customers paying through Electronic Check demonstrated higher churn rates than other payment methods, indicating potential differences in engagement and customer behavior.

### Strong Active Customer Base Remains

Despite churn challenges, the company retains more than 5,000 active customers, providing opportunities to strengthen loyalty and expand customer lifetime value.

## Recommendations

### Encourage Long-Term Contracts

Offer discounts, loyalty rewards, and contract incentives to encourage customers to move from Month-to-Month plans to longer-term agreements.

### Investigate Fiber Optic Customer Experience

Review service quality, pricing structures, customer support processes, and competitor offerings to address churn within the Fiber Optic segment.

### Promote Automatic Payment Methods

Encourage customers to adopt automated payment options through incentives and convenience-focused programs.

### Implement Proactive Retention Campaigns

Identify high-risk customers early and provide personalized offers, support, and engagement initiatives before cancellation occurs.

### Monitor Retention Metrics Regularly

Track churn rates, customer lifetime trends, contract performance, and service quality indicators to support continuous retention improvement.
